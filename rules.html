Game Format

Three 14-minute games per match. Each game victory is worth one point toward standings.

Game 1

Regular

Standard back-and-forth basketball.

Game 2

FIBA Rules

No checking the ball in after scoring. Regular back-and-forth play.

Game 3

Make-It-Take-It

The scoring team retains possession after each made basket.

League Rules

  • Scoring is by twos and threes.
  • All changes of possession must be taken back beyond the three-point line.
  • Shoot a three-pointer to start the first game. After that, the losing team from the previous game starts with possession for the new game.
  • All three games are played per match regardless of score.
  • With one minute left on the clock, if the lead is ten points or less, any foul results in a stopped clock and two automatic free throws. If fouled on a made basket, no free throws are awarded. No rebound needed — the opposing team automatically gets possession after both free throws and takes it out on top. The clock restarts when the opposing team inbounds the ball.
  • The accumulation of points determines the playoff bracket. Wins matter throughout seasonal play.
